DATABASE: Arrest-related deaths in New Jersey

Who dies in N.J. arrest-related deaths, and how?

An NJ Advance Media analysis of arrest-related deaths in New Jersey during the past decade revealed:

  • African-Americans made up 55 percent of deaths.
  • More than half of all cases were homicide by police, mostly involving suspects who were shot.
  • Newark Police Department had the most, with 19 deaths, more than twice as many as Jersey City and Elizabeth. The State Police, with a larger force and coverage area, had 15 deaths.
  • In more than half of the cases in which police killed a suspect, the person either discharged or displayed some kind of weapon. However, there were 14 incidents in which the person was unarmed. One case involved a 21-year-old motorist shot five times by Denville police.

About the data

Whenever someone dies at the hands of the police, authorities in every state are required to fill out a report detailing the circumstances of the death and submit it to the federal Bureau of Justice Statistics. Using the state Open Public Records Act, NJ Advance Media obtained 10 years of arrest-related death records for New Jersey and compiled them into a searchable database. The records include deaths in holding cells, but not deaths that occured in correctional facilities.

The terms and phrases in the database largely mirror those used by the federal government. For example, the term “homicide by law enforcement” comes directly from the records. As part of its analysis, NJ Advance Media divided the cases into six categories according to the manner of death to make it easier to search.

* Note: There were two cases that were not categorized by police as “excited delirium” cases but did involve the deceased suffering some kind of delirium. They were categorized as “excited delirium” cases and denoted with an asterisk.
